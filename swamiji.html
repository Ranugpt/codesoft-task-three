Swami Vivekananda

Indian Hindu monk and philosopher

Biography

Swami Vivekananda (1863â€“1902) was a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. He was a disciple of the Indian mystic Ramakrishna Paramahansa and was a major force in the revival of Hinduism in India.

Vivekananda founded the Ramakrishna Math and the Ramakrishna Mission. He is perhaps best known for his speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893, where he introduced Hinduism to the West with his inspiring message of tolerance and universal acceptance.

His teachings emphasized the unity of the soul with the divine and the importance of service to humanity. He advocated for the upliftment of the masses, particularly through education.
